This app is a generalization of the e-mission open source platform (https://github.com/e-mission/) intended to support research studies around travel behavior. While the initial platform development was at the University of California, Berkeley, this app is no longer affliated with the university. It is provided by the primary developer (K. Shankari, shankari@damajash.org) as a service to the research community.
This app automatically, in the background, tracks your location, accelerometer, device-generated activity recognition and battery usage. It uses this data to automatically generate a travel diary and display it to you. It also allows you to optionally annotate the travel diary with richer details or with experience information. When you launch the app, it logs information about your phone type (android/iOS), make, model and version. This metadata helps the developer to correlate app problems with specific phones (e.g. https://dontkillmyapp.com/) Since our code is open source, you can inspect the data the app collects in our repo.
To ensure proper handling of your data, the primary developer (K. Shankari) requires that researchers submit an approved Institutional Review Board (IRB) protocol before providing them with access to the app. The consent document for the study you are joining will outline the study-specific data handling and sharing procedures. Please read it carefully before accepting it. Background tracking will start only after you accept the study-specific consent document. The researcher conducting the study may choose to compensate you for your time, as described in the consent document.
If you have any questions about this app, please contact the lead developer (K. Shankari, shankari@damajash.org). If you have questions about the study you are participating in, please contact the researcher who provided you with the QR code. If you agree to join the study, scan the QR code or the link below to proceed. If you do not wish to join the study, please uninstall the app.
